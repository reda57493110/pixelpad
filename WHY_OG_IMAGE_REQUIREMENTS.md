## üìê Why 1200 x 630 Pixels?

### 1. **Facebook & Social Media Standards**
- **Facebook** requires: 1200x630px (1.91:1 ratio)
- **Twitter** recommends: 1200x630px for large cards
- **LinkedIn** uses: 1200x627px (very similar)
- **Most platforms** use this size as standard

### 2. **Display Quality**
- **Too small**: Looks blurry on social media
- **Too large**: Slow loading, wastes bandwidth
- **Wrong ratio**: Gets cropped awkwardly
- **1200x630**: Perfect balance of quality and performance

### 3. **SEO Benefits**
- ‚úÖ **Better click-through rates**: Professional previews attract more clicks
- ‚úÖ **Brand recognition**: Consistent branding across all shares
- ‚úÖ **Social signals**: More shares = better SEO ranking
- ‚úÖ **Rich previews**: Shows your brand, not just a random product

## üñºÔ∏è Product Images vs Open Graph Images

### **Product Images** (What you have on your website)
- **Purpose**: Show individual products
- **Size**: Varies (could be 800x600, 1000x1000, etc.)
- **Content**: Specific product photo
- **Used for**: Product pages, galleries, cart

### **Open Graph Image** (What you need for SEO)
- **Purpose**: Represent your **entire website/brand**
- **Size**: **Must be 1200x630** (exact)
- **Content**: Your logo + brand name + tagline
- **Used for**: When someone shares ANY page from your site
